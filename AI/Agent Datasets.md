Here are some top sources for usable data files to build an AI knowledge base for your agent:

- **Kaggle Datasets**: Extensive collection of datasets across many domains for AI and machine learning projects.  
  https://www.kaggle.com/datasets [1]

- **Hugging Face Datasets**: Open source and community-contributed datasets, especially strong in NLP and multimodal data.  
  https://huggingface.co/datasets [2]

- **Common Corpus on Hugging Face**: Large multilingual text corpus in the public domain for training language models.  
  https://huggingface.co/blog/Pclanglais/common-corpus [3]

- **Stanford Question Answering Dataset (SQuAD)**: A widely used dataset of questions and answers based on Wikipedia articles.  
  https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/stanfordu/stanford-question-answering-dataset [4]

- **OpenML**: A platform with standardized AI-ready datasets covering classification, regression, and more.  
  https://www.openml.org [5]

- **UCI Machine Learning Repository**: Repository of numerous structured datasets suitable for machine learning and AI experiments.  
  https://archive.ics.uci.edu [6]

- **GitHub Large QA Datasets Collection**: Large question answering datasets useful for NLP and conversational agents.  
  https://github.com/ad-freiburg/large-qa-datasets [7]

- **Google Dataset Search**: Search engine to find diverse datasets from across the web.  
  https://datasetsearch.research.google.com (general resource, not web result)

- **Data. Gov**: US government open datasets covering many sectors.  
  https://www.data.gov [8]

These datasets cover varied formats and content types such as text corpora, structured tables, and Q&A pairs, suitable for building a comprehensive AI knowledge base and training data indexing. Many support open use for research and development [1][2][3][7][4][6].
